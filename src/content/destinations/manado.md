Manado is where serious divers come when they've run out of superlatives. The Bunaken Marine National Park, thirty minutes offshore by speedboat, contains one of the most biodiverse coral reef ecosystems on the planet — a wall system dropping hundreds of metres with visibility that sometimes exceeds 40 metres and species counts that marine biologists are still cataloguing.

The walls of Lekuan and Sachiko's Point don't announce themselves. You drop into blue water and the reef wall materialises beside you, festooned with sea fans the size of solar panels, Napoleon wrasse the size of coffee tables, and hawksbill turtles that have learned to ignore divers. A single wall dive here can deliver sightings that a week elsewhere might not produce.

Inland, the Minahasan highlands around Manado carry a culture that feels entirely distinct from anything else in Indonesia. This is a predominantly Christian region with its own language, architectural traditions, and food culture. The Tomohon market is a genuine introduction to how extreme Indonesian food culture can get — but the same city also produces some of the finest smoked fish and spiced preparations in the archipelago.

Come for the diving. Stay for the discovery that Sulawesi operates by rules entirely its own.
